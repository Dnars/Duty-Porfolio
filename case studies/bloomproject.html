ABOUT

Bloom is a mobile application that enables pregnant woman in Nigeria get access to adequate health care services nearby.

Target Audience – Women within childbearing age.

Duration – 8 weeks.

My Role – Team lead, User experience designer, User researcher, user interface designer, visual designer.

PROJECT OVERVIEW

With the continous increase of maternal death of pregnant women in Nigeria, Pregnant women are still not accessing the adequate health care services offered by the country. The challange faced by these women has resulted to miscarriage and loss of lives of both mother and child.

THE PROBLEM

Every single day, Nigeria losses about 145 women within the childbearing age, making it the second largest contributor to maternal mortality in the world according to UNICEF. The Near-Miss and the Maternal Death survey reveals that the delay in accessing the adequate level of care plays a major role to its occurrence and maternal death.

The goal is to offer a solution that makes adequate health care services accessible for maternal patients in Nigeria.

DESIGN PROCESS

We adpoted the design thinking approach to problem solving in coming up with solutions that are centered around the users.

USER RESEARCH

Assumptions

Before interviewing users, we gathered some secondary data about the problem and came up with the assumption that the delay of patients in accessing adequate health care services in Nigeria are due to:

  • Distance of pregnant women from the healthcare centers.
  • Cultural Barrier as a result of tradition being practiced
  • Illiteracy

RESEARCH METHOD

We conducted an in person qualitative research to get deep insight about the problem, which prompted us to recruit 6 willing participants all of whom were women within childbearing age. Timeline for the interview was 3 days and we found our target audience at the health center, shops, churches, marketplace and gyms.

Research Goal While planning our research process, our major research goal was to find out the challenges faced by pregnant women in accessing adequate health care services in Nigeria and how they felt about this problem.

At the end of the research, we want to find answers to the problems pregnant women are facing in terms of getting access to adequate health care services in Nigeria and how we could help them solve the challenge.

INTERVIEW FEEDBACK

EMPATHY MAPPING

From the response we gathered during the interview stage, we created an empathy map to help us understand what our users are saying, their thought, how they felt and what they do.

AFFINITY MAPPING

We organized the insight we got from the empathy mapp into an affinity map and grouped them into similar themes to enable us identify patterns to the problems our target audience are facing. We categorized the patterns we observed into;

Goals – What our users what to achieve

  • See a doctor on time
  • Do checkup without the stress of walking under bad weather condition.
  • Get delivered by a midwife or in a church.
  • Arrive at the hospital without haste to avoid having high blood pressure.

Frustrations due to time

  • Waits for a long time before being attended to.
  • Doctors comes to the hospital at their convenient time.

Frustrations due to distance

  • Hospital is not within the environment
  • Delivered in a maternal home because her husband wasn’t around to drive her to the hospital.

Frustrations due to religious belief

  • Forced to visit the hospital when she had difficulty giving birth.
  • No experienced health personnel to help her give birth at home.

FINDINGS & INSIGHT

After mapping out similar patterns, we discover that pregnant women:

  • Hates when their time is being wasted at the hospital because healthcare personnel are mostly not on sit on time.
  • Leave their houses very early to go for antenatal due to the far distance to the hospital so they could pick up attendance number on time to be attended to according to the time of arrival.
  • Avoid going to the hospital because it goes against their religious belief as they consider it sinful doing what their doctrine kick against.
  • Finds it inconvenient leaving their houses for antenatal because of their heavy condition and unfavourable weather.

USER PERSONA

PROBLEM STATEMENT

Pregnant women needs a way to access maternal care without inconveniences because of their delicate condition which makes them easily stressed.

Pregnant women needs a way to reach healthcare personnel quickly because the distance from their houses to the hospital is far which makes it difficult for them to easily access maternal care in cases of emergency.

Pregnant women needs a way to be attended to on time because they have to wait for a long time before seeing a healthcare personnel despite being at the hospital early.

How might we help reduce the time wastage faced by pregnant women before being attended to at the hospital?

HOW MIGHT WE QUESTIONS

After drafting out a clear problem statement, we began to question how we might solve the problems we have outlined. Some of these questions include:

  • How might we provide accessible health care services without inconveniences?
  • How might we help pregnant women reach healthcare personnel quickly?
  • How might we help pregnant women delivery safely by a healthcare personnel without going against their religious belief?
  • How might we help reduce the time wastage faced by pregnant women before being attended to at the hospital?

INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE

HIGH FIDELITY DESIGN

From the insights we got during the usability study, we created our high fidelity design bearing in mind the challenges users encountered.

HIGH FIDELITY DESIGN TESTING

After we have implemented the feedback gotten during the initial usability testing via wireframe, we went ahead to test the high fidelity design to evaluate our solution.

Testing Insight

Most of the participants that tested the solution got stuck on the request home checkup screen.

“WHY?”

While trying to book an appointment with a healthcare personnel/hospital, the calendar contains two colors. A color indicating that a day/time is filled, and a color indicating the current day. Participants couldn’t quite figure out what these colors meant.

ITERATION BASE ON ACCESSIBILITY & INCLUSION

TAKEAWAY

Given our initial assumptions, we validated that pregnant women are not accessing the adequate level of health care services in Nigeria due to the distance from hospital, their religious belief, time and inconveniences they encounter.

We offered a mobile solution to help pregnant women book appointment with a health care personnel/hospital close-by at their own convenience, request for home checkup and possibly put a call through in times of emergencies. This way, we are able to significantly reduce the level of maternal mortality rate in Nigeria if not by 30%.

While it might be difficult to onboarding health care expert on the platform, we introduced a revenue model that will enable expert(doctors,nurses,midwives among others) earn extra cash while doing the things they are passionate about. Something like a freelance for medical expert.

What went wrong?

Where we failed during the execution of this project, was us not considering the context of use for each stakeholders. Taking into consideration hospital/health centers/maternity homes, they are all likely to use a desktop most of the time. Instead of designing a mobile application, we should have opt-in for a web or a desktop version of the product.

What next?

We will be reviewing the end-to-end process of this project again to capture what we might have missed and also work on the web/desktop view of the hospital solution.
